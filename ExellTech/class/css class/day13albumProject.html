About

Nature is an inherent character or constitution,[1] particularly of the ecosphere or the universe as a whole. In this general sense nature refers to the laws, elements and phenomena of the physical world, including life. Although humans are part of nature, human activity or humans as a whole are often described as at times at odds, or outright separate and even superior to nature.

Nature
Types of nature

Nature encompasses a vast array of phenomena and elements that can be categorized in various ways, reflecting the diversity and complexity of the natural world. Ecosystems, for instance, can be broadly classified into terrestrial and aquatic systems. Terrestrial ecosystems include forests, deserts, grasslands, and tundras, each with its own unique flora and fauna. Aquatic ecosystems are divided into freshwater bodies such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, and marine environments including oceans, coral reefs, and estuaries.
